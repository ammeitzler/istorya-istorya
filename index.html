It is archival, verbal, shared. It is finding reference points in conversation, in dialogue.
It is the sound of lumpia frying in the kitchen, smells of adobo in the air before a party.
It is the sound of your lola laughing, chairs squeaking closer to the dinner table.
It is the tsismis around your aunties mahjong table.
It is the whole family doing the cha-cha to “Todo, Todo, Todo” on the dance floor.
It is dad drunk off of San Miguel, dancing off rhythm in your backyard.
It is your parents forcing you to sing “The Greatest Love of All” on karaoke.
It is asking for a translation and your mom shrugging “it doesn't translate.”
It is honoring the ancestral narratives that exist within us.

Pilipinx American artists Alexis Convento (Berlin), Angeline Marie Michael Meitzler (NYC) and Jay Carlon (Los Angeles) come together to reconnect to their motherland as istorya-istorya.

@istorya.istorya

The impact of COVID-19 has shifted the dynamics of the Filipino dining experience, an essential component to the vitality and preservation of a cultural identity. With Shared Meal, Pilipinx American collective istorya-istorya will work with Visayan organizers to form initiatives that bring a spirited energy to the reenvisioning of a Kamayan dinner and the exchange of goods. Within our diasporic, ancestral and local communities, we hope to nurture and sustain new avenues of being together.
